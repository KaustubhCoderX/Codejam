The OceanSat mission, marked a significant leap in oceanographic research and Earth observation. Launched on May 26, 1999, OceanSat aimed to gather crucial data about the world's oceans, contributing to our understanding of marine ecosystems, weather patterns, and climate dynamics. The mission spanned several years and witnessed the launch of multiple satellites within the series, with OceanSat-2 being the most notable.

The launch vehicles employed were primarily from the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) family, ensuring the precise deployment of the satellites into their designated orbits. These satellites were equipped with specialized sensors to monitor ocean surface parameters such as sea surface temperature, chlorophyll concentration, and wind patterns.

Roles and roles

The OceanSat mission played a crucial role in advancing our knowledge of oceanic processes and their influence on global climate systems. By providing comprehensive oceanographic data, these satellites facilitated applications in fisheries, coastal management, and weather forecasting.
