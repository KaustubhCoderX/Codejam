The Cartosat mission, initiated by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), marked a remarkable stride in Earth observation. Launched on May 5, 2005, this mission aimed to provide comprehensive and high-resolution imagery, driving advancements in cartography, urban planning, and rural development. Spanning over the course of several years, the mission culminated with the deployment of Cartosat-2E on February 15, 2017.

2005

Cartosat mission's first satellite, Cartosat 1 was launched.

2007-2016

Satellites from Cartosat 2 to Cartosat 2D was launched, This helped in earth observation.

2007

Cartosat series came to end with the launch of Cartosat-2E

PSLV : Old yet reliable

The launch vehicles employed for the various Cartosat missions were primarily the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) series. These reliable launch vehicles ensured the successful deployment of the satellites into their intended orbits.

Payload is the key

The payloads carried by the Cartosat satellites included state-of-the-art imaging instruments that enabled the capture of detailed and accurate images of the Earth's surface. These images proved invaluable for diverse applications, ranging from resource management to disaster monitoring.
